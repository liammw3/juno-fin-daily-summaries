Executive Summary

On March 26, 2026, Juno's support queue received 33 total conversations, 15 of which (45%) were handled by Fin AI. Of those Fin sessions, 3 reached confirmed resolution, 6 were assumed resolved after no follow-up, and 6 required escalation to a human agent — yielding a combined resolution rate of 60% and an escalation rate of 40%. No Fin answer loops were detected, meaning Fin did not repeat the same response three or more times without resolution in any session.

The most common Fin-handled topics were K-1 and export-related questions (QBI to CCH, K1x limits, 1099-B straddle processing), document management issues (empty PDF folders, wash sales import), and product capability inquiries (3115 review support, document prep timelines). Fin performed well on knowledge-base-grounded questions, particularly the QBI export behavior and the K1x trial document limit, both of which resulted in confirmed resolutions within 2–3 minutes of the initial message.

The 6 escalations were driven by a mix of user-initiated live-chat requests (1 case), a critical data discrepancy bug where one client's data appeared in another client's return (1 case requiring urgent human review), and 4 genuine knowledge gaps or product questions that Fin could not fully answer from available documentation (CCH Re-Sync behavior, empty PDF display bug, W-2 import issue, CCH schedule mapping). The data discrepancy conversation (Jim Hayden, Rodriguez/Thanasides client data cross-contamination) stands out as the most significant escalation and warrants engineering follow-up.

Of the 18 non-Fin conversations, most involved complex product issues, subscription account problems, and multi-step review workflows. These are predominantly still open, consistent with the more involved nature of human-handled tickets. Average close time for Fin-resolved sessions was 6.6 minutes — indicating fast, efficient resolution when Fin could answer from its knowledge base.

Documentation Gaps

QBI & Ultra-Complex K-1 Items → CCH Export Behavior
Two separate conversations touched on the fact that QBI and other ultra-complex K-1 data extracted by Juno does not flow into the CCH export/import. This is expected behavior documented in "Juno K-1 Processing Logic Details," but users are surprised by it. Suggested fix: Add a prominent callout box to the K-1 and Binder articles explicitly listing which items require manual CCH entry after export, with a checklist format.
PDF Folder Visibility — Troubleshooting Guide Missing
Kelly McDonald reported that PDF folders showed as empty even though documents were validated and viewable elsewhere. Fin had no specific article to cite and fell back on generic troubleshooting. Suggested fix: Create a dedicated KB article "Why are my PDF folders empty?" covering: cache refresh, document archiving, and the difference between validation view and PDF bundle view.
K1x Document Limit Not Surfaced During Trial Onboarding
Rich Moring said he did not see the 25/5-document K1x limit during his trial. He selected a 38-document return before encountering the limit. Suggested fix: Add an in-product tooltip or warning before document upload during trial that notes the 5 complex K-1 limit, and update the trial onboarding email sequence to reference this constraint.
CCH Re-Sync Button — Trigger Conditions Undocumented
Jim Hayden's escalated conversation involved confusion about the Re-Sync button in the CCH Axcess export screen. Fin explained it only appears after a client update in CCH since last sync — but this behavior has no dedicated documentation. Suggested fix: Add a section to the CCH Axcess integration article explaining Re-Sync trigger conditions and when it will/won't appear.
3115 (Application for Change in Accounting Method) Capability
A user asked whether Juno can review a 3115. This is a complex, specialized tax form. If Juno has limitations here, this should be documented clearly. Suggested fix: Add 3115 explicitly to the "Using Juno Effectively" article under limitations, or create a new FAQ entry covering which specialized/amended return forms Fin can and cannot assist with.
